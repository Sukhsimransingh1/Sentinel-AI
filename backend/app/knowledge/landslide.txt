LANDSLIDE SAFETY GUIDELINES

A landslide is the movement of rock, soil, or debris down a slope caused by rainfall, earthquakes, erosion, or human activity.

BEFORE A LANDSLIDE

- Avoid building near steep slopes.
- Monitor weather forecasts during heavy rainfall.
- Identify evacuation routes.
- Prepare an emergency kit.

WARNING SIGNS

- Cracks appearing in the ground.
- Tilting trees or poles.
- Unusual sounds from hillsides.
- Sudden changes in water flow.
- Falling rocks or debris.

IMMEDIATE ACTIONS

- Evacuate immediately.
- Move perpendicular to the slide path if possible.
- Warn nearby people.
- Contact emergency services.

IF INDOORS

- Leave the building immediately.
- Avoid windows.
- Move to higher and safer ground.

IF DRIVING

- Stop immediately if debris is visible.
- Turn around if safe.
- Never drive across an active landslide.

AFTER A LANDSLIDE

- Stay away from affected areas.
- Watch for secondary slides.
- Check injured persons safely.
- Report damaged utilities.
- Follow instructions from authorities.

FIRST AID

- Control bleeding.
- Immobilize fractures.
- Treat for shock.
- Seek medical assistance quickly.

THINGS NOT TO DO

- Do not enter unstable areas.
- Do not ignore evacuation orders.
- Do not drive through debris.
- Do not touch damaged power lines.
