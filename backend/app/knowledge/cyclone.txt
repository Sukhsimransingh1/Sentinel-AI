CYCLONE SAFETY GUIDELINES

A cyclone is a powerful storm with strong winds and heavy rainfall that can cause flooding, storm surges, landslides, and destruction of infrastructure.

BEFORE A CYCLONE

- Monitor official weather forecasts and warnings.
- Prepare an emergency kit containing water, food, medicines, flashlight, batteries, power bank, important documents, and first aid supplies.
- Charge all electronic devices.
- Secure doors, windows, and loose outdoor objects.
- Trim weak tree branches near your home.
- Know the nearest evacuation shelter and evacuation route.
- Store enough drinking water for at least three days.

DURING A CYCLONE

- Stay indoors and away from windows.
- Do not go outside until authorities declare it safe.
- Disconnect electrical appliances if flooding is expected.
- Keep emergency supplies nearby.
- Listen to official government announcements through radio or mobile alerts.
- Avoid using candles due to fire hazards; use flashlights instead.
- If evacuation is ordered, leave immediately.

IF YOU ARE OUTSIDE

- Seek shelter in a sturdy building.
- Stay away from trees, electric poles, billboards, and construction sites.
- Never take shelter under bridges or unstable structures.

IF YOU ARE DRIVING

- Stop the vehicle in a safe place.
- Avoid flooded roads.
- Do not drive through standing or moving water.
- Wait until weather conditions improve.

AFTER THE CYCLONE

- Watch out for damaged buildings and fallen power lines.
- Do not touch electrical wires.
- Avoid floodwater as it may be contaminated.
- Check on neighbors, elderly people, and children.
- Report emergencies to local authorities.
- Boil drinking water if contamination is suspected.

FIRST AID

- Treat bleeding with direct pressure.
- Clean wounds using clean water.
- Seek medical attention for serious injuries.
- Watch for signs of hypothermia or dehydration.

THINGS NOT TO DO

- Do not ignore evacuation orders.
- Do not spread rumors.
- Do not drive through floodwaters.
- Do not touch damaged electrical equipment.
- Do not return home until authorities approve.
