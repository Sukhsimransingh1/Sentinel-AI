TSUNAMI SAFETY GUIDELINES

A tsunami is a series of powerful ocean waves usually caused by underwater earthquakes, volcanic eruptions, or landslides.

BEFORE A TSUNAMI

- Learn whether your area is in a tsunami hazard zone.
- Know evacuation routes to higher ground.
- Prepare an emergency kit with food, water, medicines, flashlight, batteries, and important documents.
- Sign up for local emergency alerts.
- Practice evacuation drills with family members.

WARNING SIGNS

- Strong or long-lasting earthquake near the coast.
- Sudden rise or fall in sea level.
- Loud roaring sound coming from the ocean.
- Official tsunami warning issued by authorities.

IMMEDIATE ACTIONS

- Evacuate immediately to higher ground.
- Move as far inland as possible.
- Follow official evacuation routes.
- Take your emergency kit if it is readily available.
- Help children, elderly people, and disabled individuals.

IF YOU ARE ON THE BEACH

- Leave the beach immediately.
- Do not stop to watch the waves.
- Do not collect belongings.
- Run to higher ground.

IF YOU ARE IN A BOAT

- If already in deep water, remain offshore if instructed by authorities.
- If near shore, immediately head to land and evacuate.

DURING A TSUNAMI

- Stay away from beaches and coastal areas.
- Listen to official announcements.
- Be prepared for multiple waves.
- The first wave may not be the largest.

AFTER THE TSUNAMI

- Return only when authorities declare the area safe.
- Avoid floodwaters.
- Stay away from damaged buildings.
- Watch for gas leaks and electrical hazards.
- Drink only safe or boiled water.
- Check on injured neighbors and family members.

FIRST AID

- Stop severe bleeding immediately.
- Perform CPR if trained and necessary.
- Clean wounds with clean water.
- Seek medical care for fractures or serious injuries.

THINGS NOT TO DO

- Do not go near the shore to watch waves.
- Do not ignore evacuation orders.
- Do not drive through floodwaters.
- Do not spread misinformation.
- Do not return home too early.
