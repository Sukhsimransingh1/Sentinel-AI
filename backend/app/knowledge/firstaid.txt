FIRST AID GUIDELINES

First aid is the immediate assistance provided to an injured or ill person before professional medical help arrives.

GENERAL PRINCIPLES

- Stay calm and assess the situation.
- Ensure the area is safe before approaching.
- Call emergency medical services immediately for serious injuries.
- Check if the person is conscious and breathing.
- Do not move the victim unnecessarily.

FOR BLEEDING

- Apply direct pressure using a clean cloth.
- Elevate the injured area if possible.
- Continue pressure until bleeding stops.
- Seek medical attention for severe bleeding.

FOR FRACTURES

- Keep the injured limb still.
- Do not attempt to straighten broken bones.
- Immobilize the area with a splint if trained.
- Seek emergency medical care.

FOR CHOKING

- Encourage coughing if the person can breathe.
- If unable to breathe, perform abdominal thrusts if trained.
- Call emergency services immediately.

FOR SHOCK

- Lay the person flat.
- Raise the legs slightly if no injury prevents it.
- Keep them warm.
- Reassure them until help arrives.

FOR ELECTRIC SHOCK

- Turn off the power source before touching the victim.
- Do not touch with bare hands if electricity is active.
- Call emergency services immediately.

DO NOT

- Give food or water to an unconscious person.
- Move someone with suspected spinal injuries.
- Apply unknown substances to wounds.
- Ignore severe bleeding or breathing difficulties.
