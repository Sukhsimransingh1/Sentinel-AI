CARDIOPULMONARY RESUSCITATION (CPR)

CPR helps maintain blood circulation and breathing until medical professionals arrive.

STEP 1

Check if the person responds.

STEP 2

Call emergency medical services immediately.

STEP 3

Check breathing.

If absent or abnormal, begin CPR.

CHEST COMPRESSIONS

- Place hands in the center of the chest.
- Push hard and fast.
- Compression depth should be approximately 5 cm in adults.
- Rate should be 100 to 120 compressions per minute.
- Allow full chest recoil.

RESCUE BREATHS

If trained:

- Give two rescue breaths after every 30 compressions.

Continue cycles until:

- Medical help arrives.
- The person starts breathing.
- You become exhausted.

FOR CHILDREN

Use one hand if appropriate.

FOR INFANTS

Use two fingers for compressions.

DO NOT

- Delay CPR while searching for equipment.
- Interrupt compressions unnecessarily.
- Perform CPR on a conscious breathing person.
